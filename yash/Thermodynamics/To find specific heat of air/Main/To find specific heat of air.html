Thermodynamics
Experiment No. 01
To determine specific heat of air

Apparatus
Blower, adapter, U-tube manometer, orifice meter, flow control valve, insulator, test pipe, electric heater, air inlet thermocouple, air outlet thermocouple


Introduction
Specific heat is the amount of heat added to raise the temperature of unit mass by1degreeCelsius. The specific heat of any substance is same in solid and liquid phase. However, depending on the process of heat transfer, the gas has two specific heats: the specificheat atconstant volume (Cv) and the specific heat at constant pressure (Cp). The normal valuesofspecific heats of air at atmospheric conditions are, CP = 1.005 kJ/kgK and CV = 0.718kJ/kgK.
The specific heats are functions of temperature. However, with the extremely high temperaturerange experienced in internal combustion and gas turbine engines one can obtain significanterrors.
The heat gained by the material is,
Q = m Cp ∆T
Thus, the specific heat, Cp = Q / mΔT
Where, Q = amount of heat added, m = mass of the object and ΔT = temperature difference


Description of Set up
The apparatus consists of a blower unit fitted with a test pipe. The test section is surrounded by an electrical heater. Two thermocouples are mounted in the air stream one at the entrance and the other at the exit of the test section to measure the air temperature. One thermocouple i smounted on the test rig to measure the atmospheric air temperature. Test pipe is connected to the delivery side of the blower along with an orifice to measure the flow of air through the pipe.
Air flow is measured with the help of this orifice meter and the water manometer fitted on the board. Input to the heater is given through a dimmerstat. It is to be noted that, only a part of thetotal heat supplied is utilized in heating the air.
Procedure
  1. Switch ON the mains.
  2. Switch ON the blower.
  3. Adjust the flow by means of gate valve to some desired difference in the manometerlevel.
  4. Switch ON the heater.
  5. Start the heating of the test section. With the help of dimmerstat, adjust desiredheatinput. Measure voltage and current with the help of voltmeter and ammeter respectively.
  6. Take readings of all the three thermocouples at the intervals of 10 minutes until thesteady state condition is reached.
  7. Note down the heat input.

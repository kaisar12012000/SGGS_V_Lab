Thermodynamics
Experiment No. 02
/
To determine the dryness fraction of wet steam by using combined seperating and throttling calorimeter

Apparatus :

  • Combined Separating and Throttling Calorimeter

    • Seperating Calorimeter :

    The working of separating calorimeter is based on the principle of separation of water particles from the wet steam. Separating calorimeter consists of two chambers. The inner chamber consists of separators or baffle plates. A sample of wet steam enters the inner chamber trough a sampling tube. To avoid throttling of steam, the control valve should be fully open. The steam reverses its direction on striking the baffle plates. This causes the water particles to separate from the wet steam. Separated water is collected at the bottom of the inner chamber which can be taken out for measurement of mass through a valve provided at the water gauge. The dry steam moves up and then down between inner and outer chambers. It is then condensed in a bucket calorimeter and weighted.
    Let
    mf = Mass of separated water,
    mg = Mass of dry steam condensed,
    x = Dryness fraction of wet steam,
    x = mg / (mg + mf)
    Limitations:

    This calorimeter gives approximate value of dryness fraction due to incomplete separation of water (moisture) from the steam

    Throttling Calorimeter :

    In throttling calorimeter, the constant enthalpy principle is used to determine the dryness fraction of steam. The steam whose quality is to be determined, is passed trough a throttling valve where it is throttled. During throttling, the enthalpy of the steam remains constant. Due to pressure drop during throttling, the steam becomes superheated. The throttled steam enters the inner chamber. Pressure gauge indicates, the pressure P1 of the steam before throttling. Due to throttling, the steam pressure drops to a very low value, P2 which is accurately measured by a water manometer. The thermometer placed in oil packet indicates the temperature of throttled steam. The outer vessel is well insulated to avoid radiation and condesation losses

    Let,

    Tsat2 = saturation temperature of steam at pressure, P2
    Tsup2 = Temperature of superheated steam
    For throttling process,
    Initial enthalpy (hwet) = Final enthalpy(hsup)
    hf1 + x . hfg1 = hg2 +Cps.(Tsup2 - Tsat2)
    x = (hg2 - hf1 + Cps (Tsup2 - Tsat2)) / hfg1
    Limitations:

    For determination of dryness fraction of wet steam, it should get superheated after throttling. For accurate result, a minimum of 5 degree superheat is required. The steam before throttling should have minimum dryness fraction so that it gets superheated after throttling. If the steam is very wet initially and the final pressure P2 is not low enough to superheat the steam, then this throttling calorimeter gives incorrect value of steam quality.

    Combined Separating and Throttling Calorimeter :


    The drawbacks of separating and throttling calorimeter can be overcome by using combined separating and throttling calorimeter. It consists of a separating and throttling calorimeters connected in series.
    In this, a sample of steam is first passed through the separating calorimeter where most of the moisture is separated and relatively dry steam is then passed through the throttling calorimeter which is then condensed. The mass of water separated from separating calorimeter and the mass of the steam condensed from the throttling calorimeter are measured. The temperature and pressure of the steam in throttling calorimeter is measured by a thermometer and manometer respectively. The required values of enthalpy are determined from the steam table.

    Let,

    x = Dryness fraction of wet steam,

    mf = Mass of the water separated in separating calorimeter,

    mg = Mass of the steam entering the throttling calorimeter,

    x1 = Dryness fraction of the steam measured by separating calorimeter,

    x2 = Dryness fraction of the steam entering the throttling calorimeter,

    For separating calorimeter, x1 = mg / (mg + mf)

    For throttling calorimeter, x2 = (hg2 â€“ hf1 + Cps(Tsup2 - Tsat2)) / hfg1

    Mass of dry steam entering the throttling calorimeter = x2.mg

    Overall dryness fraction of the steam,

    x = Mass of dry steam / Mass of total sampled steam = x2.mg / (mg + mf)
    x = (x1 . x2)

    Physical Lab Procedure :


    1. Start the boiler and supply steam to the separating and throttling calorimeter unit

    2. Start the cooling water flow through the condenser.

    3. Open steam valve and allow the steam to flow through the calorimeter to warm through the steam.

    4. Open the throttle valve and adjust to give a pressure at exhaust of about 5cm Hg measured on the manometer

    5. Drain the separating calorimeter

    6. Start the test and take readings at 2-3

    7. When a reasonable quantity of condensate is collected measure the quantity of separated water and the quantity of condensate
